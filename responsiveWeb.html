Spider monkey

Spider monkeys have super long libs and tail, so when they walk they totally look like sipers! They live in the forests of Central and South America from south Mexico to Brasil. They live in groups mainly of 15-20 monkeys, rarely 30 or 40. Aproached by human they bark like a dog! Japanese macaque

Japanese Macaque's, also known as 'snow monkeys', as some of them lives the furthes north of all the primats. Those that do live that far, where snow sits for many months, spend days in hot springs to keep themselfs warm. They live in groups where mainly females stay with usually one male leader, and other males leave once they are sexualy mature. Male Japanese Macaque's venture alone, sometimes connecting with many different groups or just hanging around by themselfs or other venturing males.

Sajmiri (Squirrel monkeys)

Squirrel monkeys live in large groups hight in the trees. At night, seeking shelter, they roll into a ball in a tight spots between branches and sleep. Their fur is multicolor, ranging from olive-oil on their back, to white on their bellies and yellowish their limbs. They're very fearfull, runing away from even the slight chance of danger.

Red-shanked douc

Red-shanked douc are endemic to Laos, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The species has been declared critically endangered by the International Union for Conservation of Nature, with the main threats being: hunting, habitat loss and pet trade. The red-shanked douc are among the most colorful primates. They are considered "Queen of primates" thanks to their distinctive and unique appearance.

King colobus

King colobus also known as the western black-and-white colobus, is the westernmost species of the group on the continent of Africa. It lives in small groups consisting of 3 to 4 females and 1 to 3 males, plus their young. These groups maintain distance from one another through territorial calling. Groups rarely encounter other groups of the same species but when they do, males engage in aggressive displays. Territorial calling is a common form of aggression but can also be a warning to the group of predators

Lar gibbon

Lar gibbon also known as the white-handed gibbon, is an endangered primate in the gibbon family, Hylobatidae. It is one of the better-known gibbons and is often kept in captivity. Family groups inhabit a firm territory, which they protect by warding off other gibbons with their calls. Each morning, the family gathers on the edge of its territory and begins a "great call", a duet between the breeding pair.
